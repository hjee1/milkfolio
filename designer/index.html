PRISM

Precision Robot for Interactive Super Microsurgery

Role
Industrial Design Intern
Organization
KIST Robogram Lab
Scope
Exterior Mockup, Branding, Video

The Problem

Post-surgery challenges for cancer, diabetes, and accident patients demand precision beyond human capability. The PRISM robot, with 4 independently moving arms in compact spaces, needed an exterior design that communicated both medical trust and cutting-edge technology.

The Process

As an intern at KIST's AI & Robotics Research Division, I led the exterior mockup design through multiple iterations — from early 3D modeling in Rhino to pre-production prototypes. The design evolved through close collaboration with engineers to balance aesthetic refinement with functional requirements like cable routing, arm clearance, and thermal management.

The Outcome

The completed prototype was exhibited at RoboWorld 2023 (KINTEX), demonstrating live surgical precision. The branding system I designed — including logo, video production, and exhibition materials — established PRISM's visual identity for the national research project.

02

Unmute

Prosumer Sound Culture Platform

Type
Service & Product Design
Focus
AI + Sound + Consumer Experience

The Problem

Everyone listens to music, but no one turns it into "their own sound." Existing AI music tools exclude user involvement, delivering only finished outputs. The everyday sounds that surround us — unrecorded, uncaptured — simply vanish.

The Concept

Unmute proposes a new "Prosumer Culture" where consumers become creators. A portable device records ambient sounds, transforms them into beats with intuitive shake gestures, and an AI engine composes personalized tracks based on mood, context, and taste.

The System

The ecosystem spans three touchpoints: a pocket-sized recording device (Recording Mode, Beat Mode, Listening Mode), a Sound Library app for managing all audio assets, and Sound Snap — a social feature for sharing daily sound-based short-form content with friends. Product prototypes were created using generative AI mockups, alongside exhibition leaflets and virtual artist album graphics.

XPense

Game Spending Management Solution

Type
App + Web + Device
Focus
Behavioral Design for Responsible Gaming

The Problem

In-game microtransaction systems are designed to encourage spending, but eliminating them isn't realistic. Gamers need tools to manage — not restrict — their spending habits.

The Solution

XPense is a three-part ecosystem: a mobile app for external spending tracking, a dual-screen desktop device for real-time control during gameplay, and a web dashboard for data analysis and visualization. The dual-screen design preserves gaming immersion while showing spending alerts on a secondary display.

Key features include actual vs. stated probability comparison for gacha items, AI-powered alternative item recommendations, customizable spending limits with haptic feedback through a detachable phone-mounted device, and a 360-degree joystick input for intuitive UI navigation.

PathGuard

Smart Floor Emergency Evacuation Guide

Award
Easy Lab Makerthon Excellence Award
Focus
Public Safety & IoT

The Problem

Building fires occur persistently year after year. Current emergency exit signs suffer from low visibility in smoke, and occupants often don't know evacuation routes. Time is critical — delayed evacuation exponentially increases risk.

The Research

Case studies of Korea's colored road guide lines (which significantly reduced accidents) and pedestrian LED floor signals in school zones validated that ground-level visual guidance is more effective than overhead signage, especially for people looking at their phones or children at lower eye levels.

The Solution

PathGuard is a smart floor-embedded LED guide system with temperature sensors. During normal operation, it provides wayfinding. When fire is detected, it automatically activates evacuation mode, dynamically routing occupants away from danger zones. The prototype was modeled using Hyundai Department Store ("The Hyundai") floor plans.

INSTIP

Universal Wearable Design for Deaf Taxi Drivers

Award
Daejeon Design Award
Focus
Inclusive Design & NUI

The Problem

Despite no legal barriers, deaf taxi drivers face social prejudice and communication difficulties. "Silent Taxi" services use back-seat tablets, but passengers report frustration with ambiguous text requests, slow message delivery, and unfamiliarity with digital interfaces.

The Solution

INSTIP is a finger-worn wearable device using Natural User Interface (NUI) principles. It sits between the thumb and middle finger, detects motion to convert handwriting gestures into text messages in real-time, and provides multi-feedback (LED alerts, vibration) for incoming passenger messages. The curved ergonomic body allows comfortable grip on the steering wheel while maintaining communication capability.

Features include a sliding cover for power/size adjustment, magnetic 360-degree angle control, a micro-motion sensor for handwriting recognition on any surface, and Bluetooth auto-connect.

-ENG

English Pronunciation Rhythm Acquisition Device

Type
Product + Service Design
Focus
EdTech & Multi-Sensory Learning

The Problem

Current pronunciation correction services fix "results" but miss the "cause." They evaluate word accuracy, but learners actually struggle with stress, length, pitch, and intonation — the continuous speech patterns that make someone sound native.

The Approach

-ENG takes a three-stage multi-sensory approach: (1) Auditory — a portable 40mm device captures ambient sounds and analyzes them into rhythm patterns; (2) Tactile — the app converts detected rhythms into vibration patterns mapped to English syllable stress; (3) Visual — scan mode identifies previously collected words in text and reactivates them through vibration and pronunciation simultaneously.

The device features a nano-suction attachment mechanism for mounting on any surface, and a built-in camera for text scanning. The brand identity captures the concept of "sound to body to new perspective."

KUPID

Korea University Portal Redesign & Mobile App

Type
UX/UI Redesign
Platform
Web + Mobile App

The Problem

The university portal is essential for student life — grades, scholarships, room reservations, attendance — yet students found it unusable. Excessive information density, inconsistent navigation, and no mobile experience left users frustrated.

The Research

User surveys revealed that most students use the portal primarily for information retrieval, yet finding specific services required navigating through deeply nested menus. A persona-driven journey map of a freshman trying to apply for scholarships uncovered critical pain points: too many steps, buried features, and poor visual hierarchy.

The Solution

The redesign centered on four pillars: primary functions separated into icon shortcuts, enhanced search, custom menus letting students build their own portal experience, and a native mobile app mirroring all web functionality. The new design features pre-login notice previews, visual grade tracking graphs, streamlined scholarship applications, and a community hub for campus dining, facilities, and announcements.

mêm mo

Community Screening Crowdfunding Platform

Type
Service Design & UX/UI
Focus
Cultural Platform & Crowdfunding

The Problem

Post-pandemic audiences crave diverse, participatory film experiences beyond multiplexes. Community screenings exist but remain inaccessible — hard to find, difficult to organize, and financially risky for hosts. Meanwhile, short films and festival rejects have no viable screening route.

The Concept

"mêm mo" (from French "même moment" — same moment) is a crowdfunding-based community screening platform. It connects audiences seeking niche content with hosts who want to screen films in unconventional spaces. The name embodies the shared time-and-space experience of community cinema.

The System

Five tabs serve distinct user journeys: Home (personalized recommendations), Explore (tag-based search), Community (discussion & promotion), Host (guided screening setup with library, venue matching, and scheduling), and My (tickets, QR entry, location maps). The platform lowers barriers for both participation and hosting.

Linkids

Experience-Based Children’s Education Brand

Type
Brand & Service Design
Scope
App, Branding, Print Collateral

The Concept

Linkids is a children's education brand built around the idea that learning happens best through experience and self-expression. The dual-mode app — Parent Mode and Child Mode — reflects this: parents see an emotion-based dashboard of their child's day, while children interact through an interface designed around seeing, feeling, and expressing.

The Brand System

The brand extends far beyond the app: kids' business cards let children express imaginary careers and identities, on-site ID cards create a sense of belonging at workshops, and branded notebooks capture "memory, imagination, and expression." Posters combine the brand's signature ring motif with playful icons representing children's boundless imagination.
